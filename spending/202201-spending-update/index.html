January 2022 Spending Updates

February 04, 2022

Happy Chinese lunar year. This year is a tiger year. Wish my family and every one be healthy and strong.

I converted to permanent employee this month. Comparing with working as contractor in last five years, I expect that the visible money coming to my bank account will be sharply decreased. I’m paid twice every month now and each paycheck is $3100 after tax. My RRSP matching will start after 3 months so I’ll receive around $500 less every month, which means my monthly after tax pay will be around $5700.

The spending is categorized into numbers shown in the following table.

  • My current monthly mortgage is $2020. Property tax is not charged in January.
  • We spend around $2500 on food. Is this too much?
  • Our cars have been paid off. I’m working from home so we don’t spend too much on gas. Thanks to the suspending the insurance of my van, I saved $100 on car insurance.
  • Gas is $156 and hydro is $304. Last year Hydro is only $236. Did we spend more electricity or water this year?
