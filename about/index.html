My Past

February 04, 2022

I’m a Chinese immigrant. The story here just covers my life after landing Canada after 2011 and they are mostly about job and financial status. I know many friends are better than me and I’m not intended to complain or show off.

I applied for Canada immigration in 2007. Back then, China was still on the way to boom and I just want to move to Canada to pursue better life. Before that, I have lived in Korea for many years and I married my wife there. I was a student and then a researcher in China and Korea. After landing in Canada, I had to give up my academic career path and became a software developer.

Lack of Canadian experience, it’s very hard for a new immigrant to land a job even though I have academic degrees. My first job was a cash job to work as programmer for a startup company. I practiced my English and adapted to the Canadian work environment in this job. I was paid 2400CAD every month. I rented a single room apartment in Toronto and the rent was a little more than 1100CAD every month. I cannot afford a car. Fortunately, my wife has a friend who has settled down in Toronto for many years. He used to drove us to shop around every year. We are very grateful for his help till now. However, my wife was pregnant right after the landing. After my daughter was born, we cannot pay for a helper.

Soon, a job agent found me for a developer role in a very small company. The ex software developer just left this company, the boss was desperate in finding a replacement. Before me, I heard he has tried a few developers from the same HR agency who found me. But nobody can fill the role. When the agent asked how much I want, I just asked for $25/hour. Although that was 10 years old, it was a very low bid. I guess the boss just wanted to try me out because this much money can hire no one in the market. At same time, my boss hired another senior guy who has more Canadian experiences. After 3 months, I was hired as a permanent employee. My salary was 50K to start with. I asked to raise my salary to 55K after one year and successfully raised my salary to 60K in another year.

This small company is located at Hamilton. I bought a car right after I got this job. However it’s impossible for me to commute one-way 90Kms every day. Once my boss asked me to come to the office for urgency on Saturday, I literally fell asleep when I was driving on QEW. So I moved my family to Hamilton. We rent a two-bed room apartment for $1050. One year after I worked in this company, I started to look for buying a house in Hamilton. The house price was still affordable in Hamilton then. Thanks to my generous parents, they gave me 50K as down payment, I bought a detached house with 320K in Hamilton. It’s a small house but my family had some good memories there.

I spent a few years in this company and I know it’s time to move on. Then, I was confident that I had enough Canadian experiences. Most importantly, I found I could not save money. Every year, I only get some money back after tax return. There is no RRSP match. How can I retire on except the house I can pay off before retirement. So I contacted several job agency and got another job soon.

At first, I hesitated to take the new role because the company only wants to offer me temporary contractor position. I don’t have too much contractor experience except the small company hired me as contractor for 3 months. I thought contractor position is less favorable than permanent. However, two factors made me take the chance. First, the company is a famous and big corporation and the hourly rate is $60/hour, which is much higher than my salary then.
