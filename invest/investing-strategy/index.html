My Investing strategy

February 05, 2022

Current Status

In January 2022, my portfolio in investment account finally reached $300K. It’s an important milestone in my journey to financial independence. I can still remember the moment I opened my TD waterhouse account in 2015. I have grown a lot from a investing newbie to a comfortable investor. I’m not saying I’m already a good investor now and obviously I have made a few mistakes and also had some lucky deals in the past.

Let’s talk about numbers. I have put $216,903 in the last 7 years in my investment account and my current portfolio total value is $322,340 (Feb 10, 2022). By using XIRR function in spreadsheet, my annual return rate is 12%.

I’m the only bread-maker at home till now. We moved from a small house to a bigger one 4 years ago. Thanks to the lowest interest rate ever, our mortgage is only $2K/month. I worked as IT contractor before and recently just converted to permanent employee this year. Frankly speaking, my take-home pay is less than before.

Strategy Review

Like other investors, I used to get ideas of buying stocks from news. When the weed stock came out, I bought some at relative low price and sold them when all media starts to talk about them. That was 1 year before marijuana legalization. Luckily, I made some money from this transaction. Then weed stock went crazy when all media started to report the legalization news. I regretted so much when I saw the stock price went much higher than my sold price. To not miss this great opportunity, I bought the same stock again at a higher price. Unexpectedly, the stock price plunged right after Canada government announced the legalization. It’s so painful to see the price was dropping every day, I decided to sell the stock. This time, the price was only half of my buying price. Ridiculously, the week stock started to pick up again after my sell because the company announced good revenue growth.

I read John Bogle’s book and was persuaded that index ETF is the best way to invest. The basic idea is to just buy and hold index ETFs. The whole book explained the idea in depth and many people agree with the strategy. I tried to follow Bogle’s advice but I couldn’t resist the temptation to buy stocks along these year. Now I end up with owning more than 50 stocks in my portfolio now. Obviously managing all these stocks are not feasible for me considering I only have limited time to watch the market and do trading in weekdays.

Among the stocks I bought, most of them are blue-chips big company in Canada though I also own some growth stocks. I also have belief in dividend investing because I do feel safe when I see cash distribution coming to my stock account. One day, if the monthly dividend can cover my expense, I don’t need to work or sell my stocks. My dream of financial independence is realized on that day. However, I don’t want to invest most of my money on individual stocks because it will cost me too much time to manage and it might be risky.

Future Strategy

I was passionate to buy low and sell high at the beginning, but soon I found that this path is not suitable for me. Well, I’m a software programmer and I believe I know the industry well. I thought I’m able to use my knowledge to buy growth stocks. But I actually missed the opportunity of Shopify. One of my friends asked my opinion on Shopify and I told him that Shopify is doing something Amazon doesn’t care about so I don’t think it’s a good investment. Another reason is that I don’t want to spend too much time on watching stocks when I’m working. As a software developer, frequent interruptions really drag down my efficiency. Lastly, my wife will start her appraisal business and I’ll help her when I have time.

My wife and I have TFSA account and each account has $60K. Assuming the invest return is 8% each year, I should have over $500K after 20 years. So combining our two accounts, we’ll have over $1M portfolio, which means around $40K dividend income. Remember that this income is tax free. My wife and I will also her defined benefit pension and my defined contribution pension from our work place. It’s safe to say that we won’t need to worry about money when we retire.

  • My TFSA: I’ll invest high quality dividend stock or ETF. XDIV is my current choice because it’s monthly distribution and low MER. Canadian only.
  • Wife’s TFSA: REIT and high quality dividend stock or ETF. Canadian only.
  • My RRSP: Growth stock or ETF. US or Canadian.
